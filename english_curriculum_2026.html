⏰ Standard 1-Hour Session Blueprint

8:30
START
🗣️ Block 1 · Speaking & Listening (15 min)
Warm-up game, guided question, or short listening task linked to this month's topic. Verbal responses only — no writing pressure.
💡 Online tip: Use chat box, emoji reactions, or screen-share pictures as props.
8:45
BREAK
☕ 2-min Transition
Announce what comes next before stopping. Student may stretch, drink water, or turn camera off briefly.
8:47
BLOCK 2
✍️ Block 2 · Vocabulary & Writing (15 min)
5–8 new words from topic + special interest. Short structured output: fill a word bank, label a picture, write 1–3 sentences using a frame.
💡 Online tip: Shared Google Slides or Jamboard as a virtual word wall.
9:02
BREAK
☕ 2-min Transition
Announce next block. Optional: student picks a sticker/emoji for the digital star chart now.
9:04
BLOCK 3
📖 Block 3 · Reading & Listening (15 min)
1 short text (50–100 words) or 1 short audio/video clip on today's topic. Answer 2–3 questions: 1 factual, 1 personal, 1 vocab-check.
💡 Online tip: Screen-share the text; highlight words together using annotation tools.
9:19
BREAK
☕ 2-min Transition
Last block! Announce the grammar task — keep it light and fun.
9:21
BLOCK 4
🔧 Block 4 · Grammar in Context (13 min)
1 grammar point only. Embedded in speaking or a 1–3 sentence writing task. Use sentence frames, not explanations. Student produces, teacher models.
💡 Online tip: Type-in-chat grammar games — student types sentences, teacher reacts.
9:34
WRAP
⭐ Wrap-Up (5 min)
Student says 1 thing they learned. Teacher adds star to chart. Preview next Thursday's topic (max 1 sentence). Say goodbye! ✋
9:39
END
🏁 Class Ends · See you next Thursday!
Total: ~65 min with transitions. Adjust blocks if student needs more time on one area.
🧠 Neurodivergent Design Notes: Always announce transitions before they happen ("In 2 minutes, we're moving to Block 2 — reading!"). Accept verbal answers in place of written ones on hard days. If focus drops, skip Block 4 and end with the star chart — completing 3 blocks is still a full session win. One earned star = one session attempted.
⭐ Special Interest (SI) Integration Guide
Adapt any lesson to the student's favourite topic for maximum motivation
✨ Why SI Integration Works

For ASD/ADHD learners, embedding a special interest ("SI") into vocabulary and speaking tasks dramatically increases engagement, reduces anxiety, and improves recall. The grammar and structure stay the same — only the nouns and verbs change to match what the student loves.

📝 Vocabulary Swap Rule
Replace 3–5 of each session's example words with SI-related words. Keep the grammar frame identical. E.g., "The [animal] is fast" → "The [character] is fast"
🗣️ Expert Effect (Speaking)
Frame the student as the teacher: "Can you explain [SI concept] to me in English?" This increases sentence complexity and fluency naturally.
📖 Reading Source Finder
Use fan wikis, Simple English Wikipedia, game guides, or YouTube subtitles as reading texts. 50–100 words max. Print or screen-share.
✍️ Writing Frame Swap
Use SI context in all writing frames: "My favourite [SI thing] is ___ because ___." Structural target stays the same.
🎮 Game-ify Grammar
Use SI scenarios in grammar tasks: "You are a [SI character]. What do you do every day?" → Simple Present practice.
⚡ Motivation Reset
If focus drops mid-session, pivot to the SI topic for 3 minutes ("Tell me one thing about [SI] in English") then return to task.
📦 Monthly SI Word Bank — Fill Before Each Month Starts

Write 8 SI-specific words the student will encounter this month. Use them in all 4 blocks throughout the month.

📌 Tip: Ask the student to help fill in these boxes at the start of each month. It builds ownership and excitement.

🌐 Online Class Setup & Tips — Every Thursday 8:30 PM
📲 Platform Setup Use Zoom, Google Meet, or Teams. Enable screen annotation so both teacher and student can draw/highlight on shared materials.
🖼️ Visual Support Prepare slides or a shared Google Doc before class. Visual schedules work better on-screen — show the 4 blocks as a checklist at the top of every slide.
💬 Chat Box as Safety Net If the student is hesitant to speak, let them type answers in the chat box first, then read it aloud. This reduces speaking pressure significantly.
📸 Camera Optional Never force camera on. A student who joins with audio only is still fully participating. Normalise this in the first session.
⏰ 8:30 PM Is Late — Pre-Wind-Down Tips Ask parents to avoid screens or stimulating activities 30 min before class. A consistent pre-class ritual (drink water, sit at desk) helps the brain prepare.
🌟 Digital Star Chart Keep a shared Google Sheet or Notion page as the running star chart. Student can see it live on screen and add their own star emoji after each session.
