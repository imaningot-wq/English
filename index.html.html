How to Use This Curriculum

This curriculum is built on evidence-based principles for neurodivergent learners. Every lesson is modular — use what works, skip what doesn't.

⏱️
15-Minute Blocks
Each activity is exactly 15 minutes. Use a visible timer (analog clock or timer app). Short bursts prevent overwhelm and match the ADHD attention window.
🎯
Special Interest Hook
Every lesson links vocabulary, grammar, or topics to the student's special interest. This activates intrinsic motivation and improves retention.
👁️
Visual Schedule First
Show the day's schedule BEFORE starting. Tick off blocks as they complete. Predictability reduces anxiety and transitions become smoother.
🔁
5-Min Breaks
After every 15-min block, a 5-minute sensory or movement break is built in. Movement re-regulates the nervous system and restores focus.
⭐
Earn Stars Daily
Each completed block earns a star on the weekly tracker. 5 stars = unlock a special interest activity or preferred break. Keep it low-pressure.
🔄
Modular by Design
Each skill (Grammar, Vocab, Speaking, Reading) is its own colour-coded block. You can rearrange days or add extra grammar days without breaking the flow.

🔵 Grammar Lessons — Detailed 15-Min Block Guide
🔵 G1 — Simple Present Tense Introduction
⏱ 15 min📘 Week 1 · Mon Block 1👤 1-to-1 or Self-Directed
🟢
0:00 – 3:00
Warm-Up & Schedule Preview
Show visual timer. Show today's 3 blocks on a card. "This is what we're doing today." Student ticks off each block as it starts.
📋
3:00 – 8:00
Teach: Pattern Cards
Show colour-coded cards: I like / He likes / They like. Point to subject → arrow → verb. Read together. No writing yet.
✏️
8:00 – 13:00
Practice: Fill the Gaps
6 sentences with missing verbs using SI topic. Student writes or selects from 3 choices. Immediate visual feedback.
⭐
13:00 – 15:00
Check & Star
Check together. "You got _/6." Student draws ⭐ on star chart. One specific positive: "Great job with 'He likes'!"
💡 SI Link: All 6 gap sentences use vocabulary from the student's special interest (see SI guide)
🔄 If stuck: Reduce to 3 sentences, allow verbal answer instead of writing
📦 Materials: Colour-coded subject cards, printed gap-fill, visual timer
🔵 G2 — Question Forms (Wh- Questions)
⏱ 15 min📘 Week 2 · Mon Block 1
🗺️
0:00 – 3:00
Visual Map: 5Ws + How
A6 card with WHO (person), WHAT (thing), WHERE (place), WHEN (time), WHY (reason), HOW (way). Colour-coded. Read each together.
❓
3:00 – 8:00
Question Build
6 question sentence starters on strips (SI topic). Student completes each: "What does [SI character] do?" Student writes ending.
🔄
8:00 – 13:00
Question ↔ Answer Match
6 question strips + 6 answer strips (shuffled). Match pairs. All about SI topic for instant motivation.
⭐
13:00 – 15:00
Check & Star
Review matched pairs. Praise correct answers specifically. Add star to chart.
💡 SI Link: Questions all reference the student's SI world — people, facts, events
🔄 If stuck: Allow student to choose their 3 favourite Wh-words only
🟣 V1 — Build Vocabulary: 5 New Words (SI Theme)
⏱ 15 min📗 Week 1 · Mon Block 2
🃏
0:00 – 3:00
Flash Card Reveal
Show each word card one at a time (SI vocab). Word → picture → say together. No definitions yet — just build familiarity.
📖
3:00 – 8:00
Meaning & Example
For each word: short definition (1 line) + 1 example sentence using the SI world. Student repeats the example sentence aloud.
🎨
8:00 – 13:00
Draw It + Write It
Student draws a quick sketch for each word (no art skill needed — stick figures OK) and writes the word next to it.
🎯
13:00 – 15:00
Quick Fire Test
Cover definitions. Point to each word picture. Student says the word. 5/5 = gold star. Below 5 = "Let's try 2 more times — you've got this!"
🎯 Recall Tip: Connect each word to a memory — "That word is like the name of [SI thing]"
📦 Materials: Vocab flashcards (print or laminate), blank paper for drawings

🟡 Speaking Lessons — Detailed 15-Min Block Guide
🟡 SP1 — Introduce Yourself (Week 1 · Thu Block 1) ⏱ 15 min · Low Pressure · Scripted Support
📋 Script Scaffold (use until not needed)
"My name is ___________."
"I am ___ years old."
"I live in ___________."
"My favourite [SI topic] is ___________."
"I like it because ___________."
⏱ 15-Minute Breakdown
  • Read script together (2 min) — point to each blank
  • Fill in blanks with student's info (3 min) — student writes
  • Practise reading aloud quietly to self (3 min)
  • Deliver to teacher/parent — once with script, once without (5 min)
  • Praise specific success: "I loved how you said [detail]" (2 min)
🟡 SP2 — Opinion Expression: "I Think X Because…" (Week 3 · Tue Block 2) ⏱ 15 min · Back-and-Forth · Confidence Builder
💬 Opinion Sentence Frames
🟡 Stating: "I think ___ is ___."
🟡 Giving reason: "I think this because ___."
🟡 Agreeing: "I agree. ___."
🟡 Disagreeing (politely): "I see your point, but ___."
🟡 Closing: "That's why I think ___."
  • Teacher models: "I think [SI thing A] is better than [B] because..." (2 min)
  • Student picks a side on a SI comparison topic (1 min)
  • Student states opinion using frame (2 min)
  • Back-and-forth: teacher asks "Why?" or "But what about...?" — student responds using frames (7 min)
  • Student summarises their opinion in one sentence (3 min)
💬 Sample SI Prompt Topics (replace with actual SI)
"[Character A] is better than [Character B]" "[Game/Topic X] should have [feature Y]" "The best [SI thing] is ___" "[Skill A] is harder than [Skill B]"
🟡 SP3 — Teach-Back: Be the Expert (Week 1, 2, 3 · Fri Block 2) ⏱ 15 min · High Motivation · Uses Special Interest
  • Student picks ONE thing they want to explain about their SI (1 min)
  • Give 3 min to prepare — jot 3 bullet points on a card (3 min)
  • Deliver explanation to teacher/parent as if they know nothing about the topic (5 min)
  • Teacher asks 2 genuine questions — student answers using full sentences (4 min)
  • Feedback: teacher names ONE thing done well + ONE thing to improve (2 min)
🌟 Why this works: Students with ASD/ADHD demonstrate significantly higher language complexity, vocabulary use, and confidence when speaking on their special interest topic compared to neutral topics (research-backed).

🟢 Reading Lessons — Activity Guides & Comprehension Frameworks

R1 — Reading for Key Facts (Week 1 · Tue Block 1)

⏱ 15 min · Passage length: 100–150 words · SI-adapted
Before reading (2 min): Preview title + 1 picture. Ask: "What do you think this is about?"
During reading (5 min): Read together or student reads silently. Student uses a highlighter to mark any interesting word or fact.
After reading (8 min): Answer the 5 questions below (visual format — point and write).
  • What is the main topic of this text?
  • Find ONE number or fact in the text. What does it mean?
  • Which word did you highlight? What does it mean?
  • Is this text about the past, present, or future?
  • Write one thing you already knew + one new thing you learned.

R2 — Fact vs Opinion (Week 3 · Wed Block 2)

⏱ 15 min · Opinion-heavy SI article or review
Quick sort (5 min): Teacher reads 6 sentences aloud. Student puts a 🟢 card on FACT and 🟡 card on OPINION.
In-text (7 min): Student reads short article (100 words). Use two highlighter colours: facts = green, opinions = yellow.
Reflection (3 min):
  • Name one FACT you found. How do you know it's a fact?
  • Name one OPINION you found. Whose opinion is it?
  • Do you agree with the opinion? Why / why not?

R3 — Sequence & Story Timeline (Week 3 · Tue Block 1)

⏱ 15 min · SI narrative or event text
Material: A 5-event story or real event narrative from the SI world (e.g., history of a franchise, character's journey, game storyline).

  • What happened FIRST in the story? (One sentence)
  • What happened at the END?

R4 — Persuasive Text Analysis (Week 4 · Tue Block 1)

⏱ 15 min · SI product review or recommendation
Target text: A short review or recommendation (100–120 words) about something connected to the SI — a game, film, book, or topic they love.
Spot the Techniques:
🔢 Statistics/numbers
e.g. "90% of fans agree…"
❤️ Emotion words
e.g. "amazing / incredible"
📣 Direct address
e.g. "You should try…"
🔁 Repetition
e.g. "best, best, best"
  • Circle one technique from the table above in the text
  • Does this text make you want to try/buy the thing? Why?
📖 Adapting ANY Text for Special Interest Integration
Step 1: Find a Real Text
Use Wikipedia, fan wikis, official websites, or YouTube captions about the SI topic. Keep it 100–150 words for 15-min sessions.
Step 2: Simplify Vocabulary
Replace difficult general words but KEEP the SI-specific vocabulary — those words are high-priority learning goals.
Step 3: Add a Visual Anchor
Add one image or diagram from the SI world. Students with ASD read significantly better when text is paired with a relevant image.

🎯 Special Interest Integration Guide

Why Special Interest Integration Works

Research consistently shows that students on the autism spectrum demonstrate deeper vocabulary acquisition, greater sentence complexity, and longer sustained engagement when learning content is linked to their special interest (PMC 2024). For students with ADHD, interest-driven content activates dopamine pathways that enhance focus and working memory during tasks that would otherwise feel aversive. This guide shows you how to adapt every lesson type to any special interest topic.

High-Impact Vocabulary Categories
🏷️ Nouns: Names of things/places/people in SI world — these become anchor words for building sentences
⚡ Verbs: Actions from SI world — especially useful for grammar practice (tenses)
🎨 Adjectives: Descriptive words that feel relevant — student already uses them mentally, making formal recall easier
🔗 Connectives: because / although / however — teach these in SI context: "I prefer X because it has…"
💡 Memory Bridge: Students with ASD often have exceptional long-term memory for SI facts. Anchor new English words to SI facts they already know: "Remember how [character] is called [X]? That word means Y in English too."
🟡 SPEAKING — Motivation Through Expertise
Low-Motivation Prompt
"Tell me about your weekend."
→ High-Motivation Prompt
"Explain to me who [SI character/person] is and why they are important."
→ Expert Frame
"I don't know anything about [SI]. Can you teach me the 3 most important things?"
🌟 Expert Effect: When the student is the expert, anxiety decreases, speaking length increases, vocabulary complexity rises, and self-confidence builds. Genuine curiosity from the teacher is the key ingredient.
🟢 READING — Finding SI Content
🌐 Wikis
Fan wikis, Wikipedia — adjust reading level by switching to "Simple English Wikipedia"
📰 News & Blogs
Topic-specific news sites, review blogs — short articles with natural conversational English
📺 Transcripts
YouTube auto-captions or official transcripts — bring in dialogue, informal register, real language
📖 Guides
Official game guides, instruction manuals, how-to articles — great for procedural writing models
📌 Curation Tip: Spend 10 minutes finding 4–5 short SI texts before the week begins. Save them in a folder. This prevents last-minute stress and keeps lesson pacing smooth.

📌 Teacher / Parent Quick Reference — Strategies at a Glance
⏱ Managing Time Blocks
  • Use a visible, analogue-style timer
  • Announce block starts: "Block 1 begins — 15 minutes"
  • Give a 2-min warning before block ends
  • Never extend a block if the student is dysregulated
  • Incomplete blocks still earn half a star
🔄 Managing Transitions
  • Show the visual schedule at the start of each day
  • Tick off blocks together when complete
  • Say what's coming NEXT before ending a block
  • Allow 1-min warning: "In one minute we move to Break"
  • Let student tick the schedule themselves
🧠 On Difficult Days
  • Do only 1 block if energy is low — still earns a star
  • Let the student choose which subject to start with
  • Verbal answers always acceptable instead of writing
  • Switch to a SI-only exploration block if needed
  • No pressure, no shame — celebrate showing up
🌟 Remember: Progress ≠ Perfection
This curriculum is a scaffold, not a script. Adapt freely. The most important thing is that the student feels safe, seen, and successful — even if only one block gets done today. A good session leaves them wanting to come back tomorrow.
